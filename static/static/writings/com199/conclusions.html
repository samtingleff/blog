The UCSD Department of Communication contains a large amount of valuable reference information and links. This information however, neglects the needs of undergraduates. The site could be used as a source of department and research information relevant to undergraduates. The department would need to increase awareness of the site - both inside and outside the UCSD community.

For the Communication site to expand in its purpose and maintain relevance to all, it should be decentralized. Bruce Jones cannot be expected to design, produce, edit, and maintain all past and future pages. His role should be changed to one of supervision for the variety of faculty, staff, graduates, and undergraduates wishing to publish. Through the use of CGI scripts and general publishing mechanisisms students would have a greater voice.

Any redesign of the Communication Department site should account for the needs of undergraduates. A new site should allow for student input through several mechanisms: a shared library of external links, a class forum, and general publishing. Undergraduates should be polled for input and informed (most are not opposed to email from the department) about possible changes to the site. The most important factor to consider in a redesign is the input of students themselves. Their needs should guide the site structure, design, and content.

A successful site redesign would model the successful architecture of Studenlink and Mass Communications at Berkeley. It would have the high-quality content of the UCSD CSE and Cognitive Science Departments. A successful design would borrow from the Literature (without the annoying status bar message) and Math Departments. Publishing on the web is always a balance. The challenge for the Communication Department is to meet the diverse needs of faculty, staff, graduates, undergraduates, and prospective students. In order to meet those needs, they must all be involved in the process.
