UCSD Department of Communication

The Communication major is not designed as a training program in advertising, journalism, production or public relations though courses are regularly offered in each of these areas. The major provides students with a solid liberal arts background necessary for graduate studies

in communication and other disciplines, and for professional work in a number of communication - related fields. In addition, students will have the opportunity to explore a variety of media including video, print, performance or computer communication media. Within the Communication curriculum are three broadly defined areas of study: Communication as a Social Force, Communication and Culture and Communication and Human Information Processing. Students take courses in each of these areas.

Communication institutions, techniques, and devices are defining features of human societies. One need only consider the impact of the printed word in early modern Europe or television in recent American culture to appreciate their power. The current globalization of television, telecommunications, and computer networks may signal an intensification of communication's scope and reach, with enormous impact upon local economies, politics, and cultures. Communication has become a field of vigorous interdisciplinary study, weaving together perspectives from the social sciences, humanities, computer science, and law. Intellectual developments in a number of areas have provided a new platform for the study of communication.
