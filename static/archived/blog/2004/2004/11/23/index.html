Succession planning at Mickey D's

Remember way back on April 19, 2004 when the CEO of McDonald's died of a heart attack? We wondered if ailments due to poor nutrition is something their succession plan takes into account.

COO Charlie Bell took over as CEO at the time. Here we are, just seven months later and Citing Cancer, Chief Resigns at McDonald's. Apparently the the diagnosis came just one month after being named CEO.

Let's see if the "more health-conscious and light alternatives" now on the menu help the new CEO, James Skinner, last any longer. This doesn't bode well for Mr. Skinner:

Both Mr. Skinner and Mr. Roberts, like Mr. Bell, are lifelong McDonald's employees. Where Mr. Bell started his McDonald's career flipping burgers and mopping floors at the age of 15, Mr. Skinner, 60, joined in 1971 as a restaurant manager trainee in Carpentersville, Ill.
