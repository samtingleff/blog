Kerala: A Case Study [thanks

Kerala: A Case Study [thanks dad]:

It (Kerala) undercuts maxims about the world we consider almost intuitive: Rich people are healthier, rich people live longer, rich people have more opportunity for education, rich people have fewer children. We know all these things to be true--and yet here is a countercase, a demographic Himalaya suddenly rising on our mental atlas. It's as if someone demonstrated in a lab that flame didn't necessarily need oxygen, or that water could freeze at 60 degrees. It demands a new chemistry to explain it, a whole new science.

They tell me this is the only place in the world in which yogurt is all natural. They say that the bacteria Lactobacillus bulgaricus grows in the wild only in Bulgaria. To make yogurt anywhere else in the world requires an 'artificial' source of this bacteria. I don't know how true this is but I can say that the yogurt here is very good.
