As an avid gamer who is in his late twenties, I am biased, however agree with the argument that the prevalence of video games is dumbing down rather than smartening up the US. Do not get me wrong, I do believe that video games have their positives. They improve hand-eye coordination and provide for greater commonality among those in society. I cannot tell you how many things I have accidentally dropped but have quickly caught in mid-air, which I attribute to my better hand eye coordination.

What this article failed to point out is the short-term immediate gratification that a video game provides. Many video games may take days even weeks of continuous play to accomplish its end goal, which in comparison to a good novel, intellectual hobby or other project. This does not translate well with work ethic and the work place.

The world is facing more and more complex problems that require patience, diligence and thoughtful consideration, skills which I do not believe video games help to create.

I also think that if a young child is allowed to play video games, the games they play should be limited to what is appropriate to enhance the mental and even physical development of the child. Parents should excercise strict discretion when determining what games are appropriate for their child.

For example, a young child should not be allowed to play games like "Grand Theft Auto," which the article mentions and allows the player to rape and murder a hooker and then steal her money. What values and skills does this game create?
